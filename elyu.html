La Union

Here's a guide for your next trip to Elyu

Let’s get going!

Known as the surfing capital of the North, many tourists come to La Union mainly to ride the strong waves and surf.

Surfing at the famous Elyu Beaches

Unleash your inner surfer and carve your way through the waves at La Union, the crowned "Surfing Capital of the North" in the Philippines! La Union boasts a surfer's paradise with a variety of breaks catering to all skill levels. For beginners, the gentle rollers of San Juan Beach provide the perfect training ground. Here, you can take your first tentative steps on a surfboard under the watchful eye of experienced instructors at one of the many surf schools lining the beach. Imagine the thrill of popping up on your board for the first time, feeling the rush of adrenaline as you glide across the crystal-clear water. As your skills progress, venture out to explore other breaks like Urbiztondo, where mellow right and left-hand waves offer longer rides. Imagine carving graceful turns on your board, feeling the spray of the ocean on your face and the exhilarating power of the wave propelling you forward.

See the historic Baluarte Watch Tower

Step back in time and immerse yourself in history with a visit to the 400-year-old Baluarte Watch Tower, a hidden gem nestled within the charming town of Luna. This sentinel of the sea stands proudly along the beach coast, a testament to the ingenuity and resilience of a bygone era. Built by the Spaniards during their colonial rule, the Baluarte served as a vigilant eye, scanning the horizon for approaching threats – pirates, invaders, anything that could disrupt the tranquility of the town. Imagine the Spanish guards perched atop the tower, squinting through the midday sun, their weathered faces etched with determination. Picture the frantic signals relayed from the watchtower during the Second World War, a crucial communication hub in the face of conflict. The very stones whisper tales of battles fought and won, of communities protected and lives saved.

Taste quality wines and fresh grapes at a local vineyard

Did you know that La Union boasts not just epic waves, but also rolling vineyards brimming with juicy grapes? Nestled in the charming municipality of Bauang lies a hidden gem for wine enthusiasts and curious explorers – a thriving grape-growing region offering a unique experience beyond the beach. Imagine yourself venturing beyond the sandy shores and bustling surf towns, following a scenic route that winds through lush green valleys. As you turn a corner, a picturesque landscape unfolds before you – row upon row of grapevines stretching towards the horizon, their emerald leaves glistening in the sun. This is your gateway to La Union's unexpected bounty – a world of delicious grapes and refreshing local wines.

Get the 5-star treatment at Thunderbird Poro Point

Escape to a world of luxury and indulgence at Thunderbird Resorts and Casinos Poro Point, your gateway to a Santorini-inspired paradise in La Union. Imagine stepping into a dream destination sculpted by the sea, where breathtaking ocean views and unparalleled service combine to create an unforgettable 5-star experience. Unwind in luxurious accommodations designed to pamper your every whim. Choose from a variety of beautifully appointed rooms, suites, or even private villas, each offering an oasis of comfort and elegance. Step out onto your private balcony and breathe in the fresh ocean air, your gaze captured by the endless expanse of the West Philippine Sea. Let the sound of gently lapping waves lull you into a state of complete relaxation.

Revisit the past at Pindangan Ruins

Step back in time and tread upon the very ground where history whispers secrets in the Pindangan Ruins. Nestled in San Fernando, La Union, these ruins are more than just remnants of stone; they are a portal to a bygone era, a silent testament to the enduring spirit of faith and the ever-changing tides of time. Imagine yourself strolling along the weathered cobblestones, their uneven surfaces whispering tales of countless footsteps that have come and gone. Feel the weight of history beneath your feet as you explore the ruins of what was once the grand San Fernando Church, the very first Catholic church established in the region in 1764. Built by Spanish missionaries, the church stood as a beacon of faith, its imposing structure a symbol of the growing influence of Catholicism in the Philippines.
