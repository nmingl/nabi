Filipino Delicacies

Immerse yourself in the vibrant tapestry of Filipino cuisine, where every dish is a testament to centuries of cultural fusion and tradition. Join us as we uncover the stories behind these culinary delights and delve into the heart of Filipino culture through its rich and diverse delicacies.

Filipino Delicacies & Drinks

Here are some Delicacies & Drinks from all over the country

Sisig

This world-famous Kapampangan dish is a sizzling plate of chopped pig's face and ears, seasoned with chilies, onions, and calamansi. It's usually served on a hot plate and is a favorite amongst pulutan (beer food).

Kare-Kare

Originating from Central Luzon, this rich and savory stew features various meats (oxtail, tripe, pork belly) and vegetables (eggplant, string beans, pechay) simmered in a thick peanut sauce. It's often served with bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side for an extra punch of flavor.

Bagnet

This deep-fried pork belly is a staple in Ilocos cuisine. It's crispy on the outside and melt-in-your-mouth tender on the inside. Bagnet is usually dipped in pinakbet (vegetable stir-fry) or bagoong.

Pinakbet

Originating from Ilocos, this healthy and flavorful dish is a medley of vegetables (eggplant, okra, string beans, tomatoes) slowly simmered in fish sauce. It's often served with bagnet or other protein dishes.

Pancit Hab-hab

Originating from Batangas, this stir-fried noodle dish is known for its vibrant orange color, which comes from annatto seeds. It's typically made with rice noodles, pork, shrimp, vegetables, and a savory peanut sauce.

Lambanog

This coconut liquor is another popular alcoholic beverage in Luzon. It comes in different flavors and can be enjoyed straight or mixed into cocktails. Lambanog is believed to have originated in the Philippines, possibly around the 16th century. The process of distilling coconut sap was likely influenced by Spanish techniques during the colonial era.

Buko Juice

This refreshing drink is made from the juice of young coconut. It's a perfect thirst-quencher on a hot day and likely originated in Southeast Asia, where coconut palms are abundant. The Philippines has a long history of utilizing coconuts for food and drink.

Taho

This warm and comforting snack is made with silken tofu, tapioca pearls, and sago pearls. It's usually served with arnibal (brown sugar syrup) for sweetness. Taho is thought to have originated in China, where similar tofu-based dishes are enjoyed.

Tapuy

This Ifugao rice wine is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice. It has a strong flavor and is often served during special occasions. Tapuy is indigenous to the Ifugao people of northern Luzon and has been produced for centuries.

Adobo

This is arguably the national dish of the Philippines. It is a stew made with meat (chicken, pork, or seafood), soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves. The meat is marinated in the sauce before being simmered until tender. Adobo is a delicious and flavorful dish that is perfect for any occasion.

Lechon

This is a whole roasted pig that is a staple at Filipino celebrations. The pig is slowly roasted over charcoal until the skin is crispy and the meat is juicy and flavorful. Lechon is a must-try for any visitor to the Philippines.

Halo-halo

This is a shaved ice dessert that is made with a variety of ingredients, including beans, fruits, jellies, and leche flan (a type of custard). It is a refreshing and delicious treat that is perfect for a hot day.

Buko Pandan

This is a pandan-flavored jelly dessert that is made with coconut milk, sugar, and agar-agar (a gelatinous substance). It is a refreshing and delicious treat that is perfect for any occasion.

Kinilaw

This is a Visayan dish made with raw seafood that is cured in vinegar, calamansi, chili peppers, onions, and ginger. It is a refreshing and flavorful dish that is perfect for a hot day.

Laing

This is a Bicolano dish made with taro leaves and coconut milk. It can be made with pork, shrimp, or other seafood. Laing is a delicious and flavorful dish that is a must-try for any visitor to Bicol.

Batchoy

This is a noodle soup dish that originated in Iloilo City. It is made with pork offal, shrimp, chicken, and egg noodles. Batchoy is a delicious and hearty dish that is perfect for a cold day.

Chicken Curry

This is a Mindanao dish that is made with chicken, coconut milk, curry powder, and other spices. It is a delicious and flavorful dish that is perfect for any meal.

Satti

This is a grilled meat dish that is popular in Mindanao. It is usually made with chicken, beef, or lamb and is marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and other spices. Satti is a delicious and savory dish that is perfect for any occasion.
