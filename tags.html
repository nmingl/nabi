Tagaytay City

Here's a guide for your next trip to Tagaytay

Places to go in Tagaytay to savor the cool climate before it’s gone!

You might have heard the news that we’ll be enjoying a few more cold weeks from the amihan. And honestly, this is great especially since Filipinos are looking for every opportunity to savor the last bit of cool weather before the blistering summer season enters!

People's Park in the Sky

If you’ve never been to Tagaytay, People’s Park in the Sky has to be on your itinerary. This historic landmark isn't just about the view, it's also a unique blend of natural beauty and a fascinating piece of Philippine history. One of the most popular Tagaytay tourist spots, People's Park in the Sky offers a bird's-eye view unlike any other. Gaze out at the sprawling highlands of Tagaytay, lush and green, dotted with pine trees. Then your eyes are drawn to the centerpiece - the iconic Taal Lake. Take in the majesty of the Taal Volcano rising from the center of the lake, a picture-perfect image that will stay with you long after your visit. But People's Park in the Sky offers more than just a scenic vista. For a mere P50 entrance fee, you'll also be stepping into a bit of Philippine history. The park was originally conceived as a grand mansion, the "Palace in the Sky," commissioned during the Marcos era. Construction began in the 1980s, but was halted due to political events. The unfinished shell of the mansion still stands today, a unique feature of the park that adds to its historical charm.

Sky Ranch

The Sky Ranch Tagaytay amusement park isn't your typical Tagaytay experience. Sure, Tagaytay is known for its breathtaking views and delicious bulalo, but Sky Ranch injects a shot of adrenaline and family fun into the mix. This bustling park caters to thrill-seekers of all ages. Little ones can shriek with delight on the colorful kiddie rides, while teenagers and adults can test their courage on heart-pounding attractions like the Super Viking and the Drop Tower. Don't miss the chance to scream your lungs out (or laugh uncontrollably) on these rides! But Sky Ranch isn't just about the adrenaline rush. It's a haven for creating lasting memories with your loved ones. Capture those picture-perfect moments against the backdrop of Taal Lake on the iconic Sky Eye ferris wheel. Soaring 200 feet above the ground, this giant observation wheel offers panoramic vistas that will leave you breathless. Imagine the cool Tagaytay breeze whipping through your hair as you take in the stunning scenery – a refreshing change from the usual Tagaytay activity of hopping from one restaurant to another.

Picnic Grove

Arguably the most popular and most iconic of all Tagaytay tourist spots is Picnic Grove. It’s where you can further enjoy Tagaytay’s cold breeze while enjoying packed food or take out at one of the attraction’s picnic huts overlooking the Taal Lake. Complete your Tagaytay escapade and try the many activities in Picnic Grove like horseback riding, zipline, and cable car rides! Picnic Grove isn't just about the picnic, though! It's a place to create lasting memories. Challenge yourself with a thrilling zipline ride, soaring through the air with breathtaking views of the lake and volcano below. For a more leisurely experience, take a scenic cable car ride, offering stunning panoramas from a different perspective. Feeling adventurous? Saddle up for a horseback riding adventure, exploring the natural beauty of Tagaytay at a relaxed pace. Whether you crave a relaxing picnic with a view, an adrenaline rush, or a bit of both, Picnic Grove has something for everyone. It's the perfect spot to experience the magic of Tagaytay and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Tagaytay Highlands

Escape the ordinary and delve into a world of luxury nestled amidst the cool Tagaytay mountains. Tagaytay Highlands isn't your typical resort – it's an experience in premier mountain living. Imagine waking up to crisp mountain air, sunlight streaming through expansive windows, and the breathtaking panorama of foggy mountaintops greeting you. This isn't a dream – it's your reality at Tagaytay Highlands. Here, you'll reside in a contemporary ski resort-inspired log cabin, a picture of rustic elegance that blends seamlessly with the natural beauty surrounding you. Step inside and be enveloped by warmth and inviting comfort. The cozy atmosphere is so captivating, it might just cast a spell on you – a delightful spell that makes leaving your cabin a challenge. But don't worry, we understand. There's something undeniably romantic about curling up with a mug of hot chocolate by the fireplace, gazing out at the mesmerizing view. Let the stress melt away as you lose yourself in the serenity of the mountains.

Bag of Beans

In the heart of Tagaytay lies a haven for coffee lovers and those seeking a taste of homey comfort – Bag of Beans. This iconic restaurant isn't just another Tagaytay eatery; it's an experience that warms the soul and tantalizes the taste buds. With a rich aroma of freshly brewed coffee wafting through the air, Bag of Beans beckons you in. Step inside and be greeted by the inviting warmth of cozy interiors, each branch boasting a unique, homey charm. Whether it's exposed brick walls adorned with local artwork or quaint furniture nestled amongst lush greenery, Bag of Beans creates an atmosphere that feels like an extension of your own living room.
